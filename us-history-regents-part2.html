SEQ 1: Document Relationships

  • 2 Paragraphs

    Structure your response in two clear paragraphs.

  • Task 1: Historical Context

    Explain the "who, what, when, where" around the documents.

  • Task 2: Relationship Between Documents

    Show how the docs are connected (cause/effect or compare/contrast).

  • Evidence

    Use at least 2 citations, 1 from each doc.

Strategy for Paragraph 2: R.A.C.E.

  • R Restate the question.

    "The relationship between these two documents is..."

  • A Answer the question.

    "...one of cause and effect, where Document 1 shows..."

  • C Cite evidence from both docs.

    "Doc 1 states '...', and Doc 2 shows '...'"

  • E Explain your evidence.

    "This proves the action in Doc 1 led to the result in Doc 2."

SEQ 2: Document Reliability

  • 2 Paragraphs

    Structure your response in two clear paragraphs.

  • Task 1: Historical Context

    Explain the "who, what, when, where" around the document.

  • Task 2: Reliability

    Judge bias, purpose and audience. Say how that affects trust.

  • Evidence

    Use at least 2 citations from Doc 2.

Strategy for Paragraph 2: R.A.C.E.

  • R Restate the question.

    "Document 2 can be judged for reliability by looking at..."

  • A Answer the question.

    "...its purpose to persuade, which makes it somewhat biased."

  • C Cite evidence from the document.

    "For example, the author uses the phrase..." (Doc 2).

  • E Explain how evidence proves the answer.

    "This shows they were speaking to a specific audience, which affects trust."
