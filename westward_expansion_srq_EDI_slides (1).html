Slide 4 of 14

U.S. History • Westward Expansion
Case File: Document 1a

Evidence Breakdown: The Buffalo

Detective's Notes

CLUE: Giant pile of buffalo hides. This is business, not food.

CLUE: Being shipped to the East which means the buffalo are not staying on the Plains.

INFERENCE: If you remove the buffalo, the Plains tribes cannot keep the same way of life.

Effect: Plains Native Americans lost an important resource and their way of life was threatened.

Case File: Document 1b

Evidence Breakdown: The Testimony

“. . . White men had found gold in the mountains around the land of the Winding Water [in Oregon]. They stole a great many horses from us... They drove off a great many of our cattle. Some white men branded our young cattle so they could claim them. We had no friends who would plead our cause before the law councils... They knew we were not strong enough to fight them.”

- Chief Joseph

Detective's Notes

CLUE: Settlers stole horses and cattle.

CLUE: Settlers branded young cattle to claim them.

CLUE: Natives had no legal help.

Effect: Native Americans lost property and were treated unfairly, which pushed them toward conflict.

Effective answer

This answer will score

Teacher model:

Two effects of the development of the West on Native Americans were the loss of the buffalo and the unfair taking of their animals. Document 1a shows that many buffalo were killed and shipped east which threatened the way Plains Indians lived. Document 1b shows that white settlers stole Native horses and cattle and even lied in law councils which harmed Native people.

Why it works

  • Two different effects.
  • Both named documents.
  • Explains the harm to Native Americans.

U.S. History • Westward Expansion
Case File: Document 2a

Evidence Breakdown: The "Empty" Land

By the mid-1880s, the biggest single area in the West... was Indian Territory.

One of these, 2 million empty and unassigned acres, was called the “Oklahoma District,” and the army was busy driving out squatters.

Congress finally bought out all Indian claims to this land.

Detective's Notes

CLUE: It was Indian land.

CLUE: White settlers wanted it.

CLUE: Government removed the Native claim.

Impact: Native Americans lost land in Indian Territory to white settlers and the government.
