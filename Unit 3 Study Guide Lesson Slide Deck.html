What “Stimulus‑Based MC” means

How questions will look on Friday

  • Stimulus = a short source: image, chart, quote, or mini‑passage.
  • Question asks what the source shows, implies, or supports.
  • Two choices may sound right; only one is best supported by evidence.

Strategy: Read the question first → scan the source for proof → eliminate answers that don’t match exact evidence → confirm the best fit.

Evidence first P.A.G.E. friendly

Open note helps, but it will not be easy

Why preparation still matters

  • Items test reasoning, not copying; you must connect facts to claims.
  • Distractors include true information that doesn’t answer the question.
  • Time is limited. If your guide isn’t organized, you’ll lose time flipping pages.
  • Strong answers name a specific line or detail from the source.
